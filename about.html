School's History

      
    • 1947 - The YLAC FREE SCHOOL

      

              The YLAC was opened to the poor parishioners of Sta. Mesa to foster functional literacy. 
            The School initially offered basic education up to Grade land 2. 
            Fr. Gil de Legaria was the First School Director.
      

      
    • 1953 - Government Recognition

      

              The school obtained Government Recognition from grade I-IV. Soon after, Grade V and VI
            were added to complete the Elementary course offered. The School’s name was chased to
            Sta. Mesa Parochial School (SMPS).
      

      
    • 1966 - The School acquired its lot and building

      

              Through the benevolence of Mrs. Albina Tuazon. Soon after, the School was granted 
            Government Recognition to offer a complete elementary course. The director then 
            was Fr. Ezequiel de Torrano and the assigned school principal was Ms. Juanita Mierr.
      

      
    • 1967 - A new school was developed

      

              During Fr. Ezequiedele Torrano’s stent to accommodate the growing number of learners.
            Classes began in the first rooms of the New Building.
            The following Capuchin Fathers took the balm of SMPS Dedors Fr. Gr De Legana, 
            Fr. Rogelio De Bedona, Fr. Exequel De Torrano, Fr. Jess Mendiza, Fr. Benardino Cumagun, 
            Fr. Angel de los Arcos, Fr. Alfredo Micua, Fr. Paulino Velasquez. Fr. Eduardo Dumaual, 
            Fr. Pete Feniz, and Fr. Mike Peralta who was responsible for introducing computers to 
            the Curriculum. The Principals who have served the School were: Mrs. Lourdes del Rosario, 
            Miss Juanita Mierr, Miss Amparo Araque.
      

      
    • 1995 - The management of the institution was turned 
             over to the Diocesan Fathers

      

              Upon the instruction of His Eminence Jaime Cardinal Sin the Archbishop of Manila. 
            The first Diocesan Priest to be appointed as Director of the School was Monsignor 
            Mario D Enriquez with Mrs. Rosie Pineda as Principal. Significant changes were initiated 
            starting with the construction and inauguration of the covered court, renovation of Offices, 
            relocation and construction of Dental and Medical Clinic, Library and School's Canteen. SMPS
            became a member of Manila Archdiocesan and Parochial Schools Association(MAPSA).
      

      
    • 1996 - New Principal

      

            Mrs. Josefina Madrid was appointed as the principal.
      

      
    • 2000 - Start of SMPS

      

              Msgr. Enriquez was succeeded by Rev. Fr. Eugenio L. Castañeda. Rev. Fr. Marion C. Munda
            was then appointed as assistant director. Another milestone was added to the history of SMPS, 
            a big decision was made by the administrators to open the High School Department to cater 
            the growing demand of the community.
      

      
    • 2003 - A four-storey building was constructed

      

              The new building was named after and inaugurated by His Eminence Jaime Cardinal Sin on 
            the first day of August, 2003. The new edifice houses the library, classrooms, science 
            laboratory, computer room, faculty area, restrooms, home economics room, canteen, 
            production and the mini grotto of our Blessed Virgin Mary. The school was granted a 
            government permit to separate its first and second year levels of the secondary course.
      

      
    • 2004 - A new building was constructed

      

            To respond to the increasing number of students, a new building was constructed.
      

      
    • 2007 - Result of the Board of Trustees resolutions

      

              As a result of the Board of Trustees resolutions, Sta. Mesa Parochial School changed
            its name to SACRED HEART OF JESUS CATHOLIC SCHOOL. It was named after 
            the patron of the parish, Sacred Heart of Jesus. SHJCS obtained its Government permit 
            to offer the complete Secondary course (1st - 4th year). The Highschool department 
            received government recognition. Rev. Fr. Marion C. Munda was appointed as interim 
            principal and Dr. Henry A. Davalos as Vice-Principal.
      

      
    • 2008 - New principal

      

            Dr. Henry A. Davalos was appointed as the school principal.
      

      
    • 2010 - New School Director and School Principal

      

              Rev. Fr. Solomon A. Jardinero was appointed as the new school director and principal 
            and Dr. Henry A. Davalos as assistant principal. Under Fr, Jardinero’s term ,the speech 
            laboratory was constructed and the science laboratory was renovated. The School applied
            for FAPE certification.
      

      
    • 2011 - New School Director, Finance Officer and School Principal

      

              Rev. Fr. Marion C. Munda was appointed as the new school director, Msgr. Mario D. Enriquez 
            as finance officer and Dr. Henry A. Davalos as school principal. The curriculum was re-aligned 
            to the school Philosophy and Vision - Mission statements. The school is preparing for 
            accreditation from the Philippines Association of Accredited School, Colleges and Universities.
      

      
    • 2012 - Status for accreditation by PAASCU

      

            The school has applied for a candidate status for accreditation by PAASCU. Consultancy visit 
            followed. K-12 was implemented to adhere to the DepEd mandate.
      

      
    • 2013 - Granted candidate status by PAASCU

      

              The school was granted candidate status by PAASCU. The school facade was renovated and
            additional classrooms were constructed to cater the increasing population of the school.
      

      
    • 2014 - Constructed a chapel

      

              The Immaculate Heart of Mary Chapel was constructed and blessed by 
            His Excellency Bishop Socrates B. Villegas.
      

      
    • 2015 - Passed the PAASCU accreditation

      

              The school passed the PAASCU accreditation and was granted Level 1 status. The school 
            was granted permit by the DepEd to offer senior High School (Grades 11 and 12). Curriculum 
            for academic track HUMSS, STEM, ABM was approved and ready for implementation.
      

      
    • 2016 - Kinder to Grade 12

      

              Continuous implementation of K-12 program, First batch of SHS (Grade 11).
      

      
    • 2017 - Additional classrooms were constructed

      

              Additional classrooms were constructed, and the gymnasium was renovated to cater the 
            needs of the SHS. Preparation for the 2nd visit of PAASCU. Continuous implementation 
            of SHS, second batch (Grade 11 and 12).
      

      
    • 2018 - First batch of SHS students graduated

      

              First batch of SHS students graduated. Offering of new Curriculum of Preschool 
            Department/Montessori Approach. The improvement of the facilities in the Preparatory  
            Department and School Canteen.
      

      
    • 2019 - Blessed the newly renovated facilities

      

              His Eminence Antonio Cardinal Tagle blessed the newly renovated School Canteen, 
            Preschool Classroom, Gymnasium and TLE laboratory. The school underwent a 
            revisit by PAASCU. The school was granted level II status by PAASCU.
      

      
    • 2020 - Covid-19 Pandemic

      

              At the onset of the Covid-19 pandemic, the school transitioned from face-to-face 
            instruction to virtual / online learning. The school offered varied learning modalities 
            like online and modular learning to ensure the continuity of learning. The school 
            continued with the improvement of its facilities by constructing the school elevator.
      

      
    • 2021 - Different modalities

      

              The school continued to offer the different modalities (Online, Modular, and Blended) 
            in the second year of the implementation of the new normal curriculum. His excellency 
            most Reverend Broderick S. Pabillo blessed the newly constructed school 
            elevator on June 11, 2021.
      

      
    • 2022 - From online to face-to-face

      

              With the transition from online to face-to-face, the school launched its flagship program 
            in curriculum, Learners’ Enhancement Academic Progression (LEAP) program. This 
            program is part of the schools’ academic innovation in consonance with Standards-Based 
            Instruction. The school was invited to apply for PAASCU Level III accreditation.
      

      
    • 2024 - The school was granted Level III status by PAASCU

      

              The school was granted Level III status by PAASCU; a statement that SHJCS continues 
            to offer quality basic Catholic education post-pandemic. With the full implementation of 
            Standards - based Instruction (Instruction, Supervision Assessment and Intervention) the 
            school introduced its post-pandemic curriculum innovation - (Revitalized and Inclusive 
            Standards-based Education).

Vision, Mission, Identity Statements


Vision


A people called by the Father in Jesus Christ
to become a community of persons with
fullness of life witnessing the Kingdom of God
by living the paschal mystery in the power of the
Holy Spirit with Mary as companion.

Mission



      Through the ever-encompassing love of the Sacred 
      Heart of Jesus, the SHJCS is committed to:

        1. Promote religion as the heart of the curriculum
           by providing a nurturing Catholic learning 
           environment and fostering Gospel values and
           well-being.

        2. Develop holistic individuals that will become
           persons of character and competence.

        3. Produce graduates who will become productive
           and responsible servant leader.

        4. Promote love of country and nationalism.

        5. Exercise efficient management and delivery of
           services.

Identity


Inflamed by the Sacred Heart of Jesus inspired
by the Apostolic Vision of the Archdiocese of
Manila and in adherence to the thrust of MAPSA,
the SHJCS envision itself as a Catholic Educational
Institution that proclaims the Gospel, forms human
persons and renews the Church and nation.


SHJCS Core Values


Service


By showing initiative and willingness to render a helping hand and not expecting anything in return. “In everything I showed you that by working hard in this manner you must help the weak and remember the words of the Lord Jesus, that He, Himself said, ‘it is more blessed to give than to receive’.”
- Acts 20:35


Humility


By accepting one’s strengths and weaknesses as well as recognizing the strengths and weaknesses of others. “He leads the humble in what is right, and teaches the humble his way.”
- Psalms 25:9

Justice


By giving everyone the possibility to enjoy a decent life worthy of becoming children of God. “Learn to do good, seek justice, correct oppression; bring justice to the fatherless, plead the widows.”
- Isaiah 1:17


Charity


By showing our special love for the poor through good deeds. “A generous man will himself be blessed, for he shares his food with the poor.”
- Proverbs 22:9

Simplicity


By being simple in everything we do in our thoughts, words and actions. “All things were made through Him, and without Him was not anything made that was made.”
- John 1:3

SHJCS Educational Philosophy


                                                            

 We believe that SACRED HEART OF JESUS CATHOLIC SCHOOL exists because of Christ the
foundation of the whole educational enterprise of our school. With religion as the core,
the principles of the Gospel, the teachings of the Church and our human experiences
are our educational norms, and the school's internal motivation and final goal.

 We believe that our students are God's children, created in His image, endowed with
gifts, talents and potentials. Hence, it is our responsibility to provide them a learning
environment that nurtures their multiple intelligence and recognizes their individual
differences for holistic development.


 We believe that all personnel, in partnership with the parents, are witnesses of service
humility justice, charity and simplicity, in teaching the minds and the transforming
the hearts of our students

 We believe that we will be of service to God and our Country by following the
examples of Jesus.

 Inspired by our devotion to the Sacred Heart of Jesus, we believe that We FORM CHRIST 
in every member of his institution.
